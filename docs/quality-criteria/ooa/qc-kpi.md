## Quality Criteria:
When defining KPIs, it is essential to ensure that they are SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. This ensures that the KPIs are clear and actionable, allowing for effective performance tracking and decision-making.
1. **Specific**: KPIs should be clear and specific, focusing on a particular area of performance. This helps to avoid ambiguity and ensures that everyone understands what is being measured.
1. **Measurable**: KPIs must be quantifiable, allowing for objective measurement and comparison over time. This enables organizations to track progress and identify trends.
1. **Achievable**: KPIs should be realistic and attainable, considering the resources and constraints of the organization. This ensures that the goals set are challenging yet feasible.
1. **Relevant**: KPIs should be aligned with the overall business objectives and strategies. This ensures that the performance indicators are meaningful and contribute to the success of the organization.
1. **Time-bound**: KPIs should have a defined time frame for achievement. This helps to create a sense of urgency and allows for timely evaluation of progress.
1. **Actionable**: KPIs should provide insights that can lead to actionable steps. This means that the data collected from KPIs should be used to make informed decisions and drive improvements in performance.
1. **Balanced**: KPIs should be balanced across different areas of the business, such as financial, customer, internal processes, and learning and growth perspectives. This ensures a comprehensive view of organizational performance.
1. **Consistent**: KPIs should be consistently defined and measured over time to allow for accurate tracking and comparison. This helps to identify trends and make informed decisions based on historical data.
1. **Communicated**: KPIs should be effectively communicated to all relevant stakeholders. This ensures that everyone understands the goals and can work towards achieving them.
1. Each KPI should have an identifier (e.g., KPI-FIN-001 for financial KPIs, KPI-CUST-001 for customer KPIs, etc.) to facilitate tracking and reference throughout the development process. And a reference to either a business objective or a specific FURPS+ criterion.
